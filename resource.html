In Africa, computer proficiency is disproportionately low due to a variety of factors such as limited resources, inadequate
infrastructure, and a shortage of professionals. This is highlighted by reports such as the World Economic Forum Network's
Global Information Technology Reports, where the first African country, South Africa, appears in 68th place, and last place went to Chad.
The lack of accessibility to resources poses a significant barrier to individuals looking to enter the field of computer science,
which is rapidly growing in demand and popularity. To address this issue, I have compiled series of websites that provides
and teachers users how to code and program. By doing so, I hope to encourage individuals in Africa and other parts of the world
looking to pursue computer science.

  1. 1. Khan Academy

    Khan Academy offers a wide range of coding courses for learners of all levels, from beginners to advanced programmers. The courses cover
    various programming languages, including JavaScript, HTML/CSS, SQL, and Processing, among others. Each course is broken down
    into manageable units, making it easy for learners to progress at their own pace. The courses on Khan Academy are designed to be interactive
    and engaging, with a mix of videos, tutorials, and hands-on exercises. Overall, the coding courses on Khan Academy provide learners with a
    comprehensive introduction to coding concepts and practical coding skills. Khan Academy is an excellent platform for anyone looking to learn
    to code.

  2. 2. The Odin Project

    The Odin Project is an exceptional online curriculum that offers a comprehensive and free web development course designed to equip learners
    with the skills and knowledge necessary to become proficient web developers. The program provides a full-stack web development program
    that covers the entire spectrum of web development, starting from the basics of HTML and CSS to advanced Ruby on Rails, JavaScript, and
    database management. The curriculum is completely self- paced and users have the chance to learn at their own pace.

  3. 3. Code.org

    Code.org is a non-profit organization that offers free online courses, curriculum materials, and professional development opportunities to
    make computer science education accessible to students of all ages and backgrounds. The platform provides interactive tutorials and games
    through its Hour of Code program, as well as a comprehensive K-12 computer science curriculum, including lesson plans and instructional
    videos. Code.org is an excellent resource for anyone wanting to learn computer science, regardless of their experience level.

  4. 4. Solo Learning

    Solo Learn is an online learning platform that offers courses in various programming languages. It features interactive and bite-sized
    lessons, quizzes, and coding challenges. It also has a social learning component and a mobile app for on-the-go learning.
    SoloLearn provides certification programs recognized by industry leaders, making it an excellent platform for learners to improve
    their coding skills and advance their careers in tech.

  5. 5. Free Code Camp

    FreeCodeCamp is a nonprofit organization that offers coding courses in web development, data science, and more. It provides a
    comprehensive curriculum, complete with projects and challenges that help you put your new coding skills to use. FreeCodeCamp
    offers a community-driven learning experience, with opportunities to connect with other learners and collaborate on projects.
    One unique feature of FreeCodeCamp is its emphasis on giving back to the community by completing real-world projects for nonprofit
    organizations.

  6. 6. W3Schools

    W3Schools is a popular online platform that offers a wide range of web development courses for beginners and advanced learners.
    The courses cover various programming languages, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and SQL, among others. Each course is designed to
    provide learners with a comprehensive understanding of web development concepts and practical skills. W3Schools courses are interactive
    and engaging, with a combination of video tutorials, examples, and hands-on exercises. They provide an excellent learning experience for anyone
    looking to improve their web development skills. With W3Schools, you can learn at your own pace and take advantage of their extensive resources
    to become a proficient web developer.
